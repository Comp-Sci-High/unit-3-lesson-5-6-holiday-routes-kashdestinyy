Roses

Roses are the most popular flower for Valentine's Day. Red roses symbolize love and passion, while pink roses represent admiration and gratitude.

Chocolates

Chocolates are a classic gift, with heart-shaped boxes being especially popular. Dark chocolate is often preferred for its health benefits.

Most Popular Gifts

  • Flowers (roses)
  • Chocolates & sweets
  • Greeting cards
  • Jewelry
  • Dinner dates

Fun Fact

Valentine's Day generates billions in sales worldwide, with a significant portion on flowers and sweets.

History of Valentine's Day

Valentine's Day originated from the Roman festival of Lupercalia and the martyrdom of Saint Valentine. It became associated with romantic love in the Middle Ages.

Other Traditions

Beyond cards and gifts, traditions include love poems, serenades, and in some cultures, exchanging locks or keys symbolizing commitment.

Global Celebrations

While popular in the West, Valentine's Day is celebrated worldwide with unique twists, like in Japan where women give chocolates to men.

Love Symbols

Hearts, doves, Cupid's arrows, and infinity symbols are common motifs representing eternal love and affection.

The Story of Valentine's Gifts

From handwritten notes to elaborate experiences, the ways we celebrate have evolved. Chocolate boxes, bouquets of roses, and heartfelt cards continue to be enduring favorites.

Tip: pairing a thoughtful card with a small box of chocolates and a single rose can be more meaningful than expensive gifts.
